# Review Checklist

Run this checklist before delivering an external or formal Chinese Word document.

## Type And Structure

- The document type matches the real intent, not only the working title
- The structure is complete for that document type
- The conclusion and closing formula are appropriate

## Terminology

- Key project names are consistent throughout
- Platform names, role names, and data terms do not drift
- The same concept is not described with multiple competing labels

## Logic

- Background, objective, path, and safeguards are in a sensible order
- Phase sequencing is explicit
- Ownership and division of responsibility are visible where needed
- Risks, constraints, and scope boundaries are stated clearly

## Formatting

- Page size and margins are consistent
- Heading hierarchy is stable
- Body font and heading font are consistent
- Tables are readable and not overloaded
- Page numbers and footer rules are coherent with the use case

## Formality

- Wording is direct and formal
- The document does not contain unnecessary meta-writing such as how the document was prepared
- Brand introductions, product sales language, and decorative slogans have been removed unless explicitly requested

## Completeness

- Dates, departments, issuers, and contact fields are correct
- Attachment references are complete and match the actual attachment list
- FAQ answers or action items are not missing from the final version

## Delivery

- The `.docx` opens correctly
- The document still renders acceptably after sharing or conversion
- If a template was provided, the final output did not drift from that template
