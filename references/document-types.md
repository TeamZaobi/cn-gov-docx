# Document Types

Use the document's intent, audience, and closing formula to choose the type.
Do not rely only on the filename.

## `briefing-report`

Use for:

- project briefings
- progress reports
- situation notes
- summary reports
- leadership briefing materials

Typical structure:

1. Title
2. Opening summary or background
3. Current status or core judgment
4. Main findings or progress
5. Problems, risks, or constraints
6. Next steps or recommendations
7. Closing note, issuer, and date if needed

Language characteristics:

- direct and evidence-based
- explicit phase and progress description
- no unnecessary brand or product digressions

## `implementation-plan`

Use for:

- construction plans
- rollout plans
- work plans
- phased implementation plans
- governance and operational plans

Typical structure:

1. Title
2. Background and basis
3. Objectives and scope
4. Overall architecture or operating model
5. Phased arrangement
6. Key tasks and division of responsibilities
7. Safeguards and support conditions
8. Closing note, attachments, and date if needed

Language characteristics:

- emphasizes sequence, scope, ownership, and milestones
- makes phased rollout and validation logic easy to follow

## `official-letter`

Use for:

- formal communication letters
- reply letters
- coordination letters
- service response letters

Typical structure:

1. Title or subject
2. Addressee
3. Background or reason for writing
4. Main message or response
5. Closing formula
6. Issuer and date

Common closing formulas:

- "特此函告。"
- "特此函复。"
- "请予支持为盼。"

## `request`

Use for:

- approval requests
- applications
- submission notes
- requests for review or endorsement

Typical structure:

1. Title
2. Addressee
3. Matter requested
4. Basis and necessity
5. Proposed arrangement
6. Requested decision
7. Closing formula
8. Issuer and date

Common closing formulas:

- "妥否，请批示。"
- "以上请审议。"
- "当否，请批复。"

## `faq`

Use for:

- common question documents
- service explanations
- onboarding or usage guides
- external-facing clarifications

Typical structure:

1. Title
2. Scope statement
3. Question and answer groups
4. Boundary or disclaimer notes
5. Contact or service channel, if needed

Language characteristics:

- question wording should match real user concerns
- answers should be concise, complete, and stable
- terminology must stay aligned with the main scheme document

## `meeting-minutes`

Use for:

- meeting minutes
- decision summaries
- coordination records
- follow-up memos

Typical structure:

1. Title
2. Time, place, participants
3. Agenda or meeting purpose
4. Discussion summary
5. Decisions made
6. Follow-up actions, owners, and deadlines
7. Recorder and date if needed

## `general-formal`

Use when the material is formal but does not fit the above categories cleanly.

Default structure:

1. Title
2. Purpose or background
3. Main content in stable heading hierarchy
4. Closing summary
5. Date or issuer when appropriate

## Selection Hints

- If the document is trying to explain progress, status, or findings, prefer `briefing-report`.
- If it is arranging tasks, stages, or safeguards, prefer `implementation-plan`.
- If it addresses another organization directly, prefer `official-letter` or `request`.
- If it translates a scheme into user-facing answers, prefer `faq`.
- If it records decisions and follow-ups, prefer `meeting-minutes`.
